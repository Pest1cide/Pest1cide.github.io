Research

Safe motion for robots in complicated worlds.

My research sits between control, motion planning, and robot learning. I focus on representations and policies that help manipulators move efficiently around obstacles without losing the structure that makes their behavior understandable.

Current focus · EPFL LASA

Obstacle avoidance with coupled dynamical systems

Under the supervision of Prof. Aude Billard and Dr. Rui Wu, I am investigating manipulability-aware diffeomorphic dynamical systems for safe and efficient robot motion. The work combines obstacle avoidance and manipulability optimization while preserving useful stability and contraction properties.

Research questions

The problems I keep returning to.

01 · Representation

What should a robot know about distance?

Exploring self-supervised configuration-space distance functions that retain useful distance and gradient information for manipulation.

02 · Control

How can safety shape motion without freezing it?

Combining dynamical systems, geometric transformations, and sampling-based control to produce responsive obstacle-aware behavior.

03 · Learning

When does learned geometry improve a policy?

Evaluating whether distance-field observations improve reinforcement-learning efficiency and collision avoidance in manipulation tasks.

How I work

Model, then interrogate the model

I use controlled simulations and explicit baselines to understand when a new representation or controller helps, and where it fails.

Connect learning to geometry

My current experiments combine neural distance fields, gradients, Eikonal-style objectives, and configuration-space reasoning.

Build the full path to evaluation

Python, C++, PyTorch, ROS, Isaac Lab, PyBullet, Genesis, MATLAB, and experiment tooling all appear when the question requires them.
