# One Robotics Path Across Three Universities

My robotics education has moved through three very different environments: SUSTech in Shenzhen, Aalto University
in Helsinki, and EPFL in Lausanne. Each one changed the questions I ask and the way I work.

## SUSTech: build the whole robot

Robotics engineering gave me a broad foundation across mechanics, electronics, control, and software. Work on a
wheel-legged RoboMaster robot made one lesson especially clear: a controller cannot ignore the machine it controls.
Simulation, dynamics, hardware verification, and parameter tuning all belong to the same loop.

## Aalto: think about systems and scale

At Aalto, my focus expanded toward autonomous systems and multi-agent planning. Research on decentralized path
planning made scalability a concrete engineering concern. Leading a Spot digital twin project also showed me how
much clear interfaces and team coordination matter when several technical domains meet.

## EPFL LASA: connect structure and learning

At LASA, I have been exploring obstacle avoidance through dynamical systems and learned distance representations.
The work encourages a balance I value: use learning where it adds expressive power, while preserving structure
that helps us reason about robot behavior.

## What connects them

Across all three places, the most interesting problems have lived between fields. That is where I want to keep
working: where control meets learning, geometry becomes motion, and a research idea has to survive implementation.
