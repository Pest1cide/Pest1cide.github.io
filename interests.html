04 Life

Beyond the lab

Places change the way I see problems. I grew into robotics across Shenzhen, Helsinki, and Lausanne — each brought a different research culture, landscape, and way of thinking. This page is a record of the world around the work: the mountains, the roads, and the photographs in between.

In the mountains

Climbing

Routes, granite, and patience. Living between Helsinki and Lausanne puts the Alps within reach, and the mountains have become the counterweight to lab hours — a place where progress is measured one hold at a time.

The proper stories — routes, crags, and lessons learned the hard way — are still being written. They will land here as they happen.

On the road

Cycling

Lakeside kilometres between Lausanne and everywhere else. The shore of Lake Geneva is hard to beat for clearing a head full of distance fields — long, quiet stretches where the only optimization problem is pacing.

Ride notes and favourite roads will fill this section as the kilometres add up.

Behind the lens

Photography

A small visual record of the world around the work: the Matterhorn above Zermatt at dusk, the EPFL campus framed by trees and mountains, Lake Geneva from the shore.

Photography creates space to notice structure, scale, and motion outside a simulator or laboratory — more frames will join these as the record grows.

A few constants

What stays with me between projects.

01

Learning by building

I understand an idea best when I can turn it into a model, run it, break it, and make the next experiment sharper.

02

Crossing disciplines

Robotics keeps me moving between mechanics, control, software, learning, and the human context around a system.

03

Looking outward

Travel and photography create space to notice structure, scale, and motion outside a simulator or laboratory.

04

Languages

Mandarin is my native language, and English is the working language of my international study and research.

05

Communities

My path has included SUSTech robotics teams, Aalto research and project groups, EPFL LASA, competitions, and hackathons.

06

Perspective

I value teams that combine technical ambition with direct communication, careful experiments, and the willingness to learn across fields.
